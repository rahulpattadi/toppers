1 Mark Physics
Based on the circuit diagram shown below, define electric current and state its SI unit.
Figure: Electric current flow in a simple circuit with conventional current direction

Solution:

Electric Current: Electric current is defined as the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor.

Formula: I = Q/t

Where: I = Current, Q = Charge, t = Time

Diagram showing conventional current flow from positive to negative terminal

SI Unit: Ampere (A)

Remember: 1 Ampere = 1 Coulomb/second

2 Marks Mathematics
Using the logarithmic properties shown in the graph below, solve: If log₂(x) = 5, find the value of x. Also, verify your answer.
Figure: Comparison of different logarithmic functions including log₂(x)

Solution:

Given: log₂(x) = 5

Step 1: Convert to exponential form

If log₂(x) = 5, then x = 2⁵

x = 2⁵ = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 32

Step 2: Verification

Check: log₂(32) = log₂(2⁵) = 5 ✓

Answer: x = 32

Key concept: log_a(x) = n means x = aⁿ

1 Mark Chemistry
Observe the molecular structure shown below. What is the molecular formula of this compound? Draw its Lewis structure.
Figure: 3D ball-and-stick model of the simplest hydrocarbon molecule

Solution:

Molecular Formula: CH₄ (Methane)

Lewis Structure:

    H
    |
H—C—H    (Bond angle: 109.5°)
    |
    H

Details:

  • 1 Carbon atom bonded to 4 Hydrogen atoms
  • Tetrahedral geometry with 109.5° bond angles
  • Simplest alkane (saturated hydrocarbon)
  • sp³ hybridization of carbon
Remember: Methane is the simplest member of the alkane family (CₙH₂ₙ₊₂)

2 Marks Biology
Study the leaf cross-section diagram below. Explain the process of photosynthesis and write the overall chemical equation.
Figure: Cross-section of a leaf showing chloroplast-containing cells where photosynthesis occurs

Solution:

Photosynthesis: The process by which green plants convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose using sunlight and chlorophyll.

Chemical Equation:

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Process Details:

  • Reactants: Carbon dioxide (CO₂), Water (H₂O), Light energy
  • Products: Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆), Oxygen (O₂)
  • Location: Chloroplasts (mainly in mesophyll cells of leaves)
  • Catalyst: Chlorophyll pigment
  • Two stages: Light reactions (thylakoids) and Calvin cycle (stroma)
Remember: Light reaction produces ATP & NADPH, Calvin cycle uses them to make glucose
