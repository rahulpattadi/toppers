Combination Reactions

A reaction in which two or more reactants combine to form a single product.

A + B → C
Burning of Coal
C(s) + O₂(g) → CO₂(g)
Formation of Water
2H₂(g) + O₂(g) → 2H₂O(l)
Quick Lime + Water → Slaked Lime
CaO(s) + H₂O(l) → Ca(OH)₂(aq)

Exothermic Reactions

Reactions in which heat is released along with the formation of products.

Burning of Natural Gas
CH₄(g) + 2O₂(g) → CO₂(g) + 2H₂O(g) + Heat
Respiration (Exothermic Reaction)
C₆H₁₂O₆(aq) + 6O₂(g) → 6CO₂(g) + 6H₂O(l) + Energy

Decomposition Reactions

A reaction in which a compound splits into two or more simple substances.

A → B + C

Thermal Decomposition

When decomposition is carried out by heating.

Ferrous Sulphate Decomposition
2FeSO₄(s) 🔥 Heat → Fe₂O₃(s) + SO₂(g) + SO₃(g)

Green colour → Red-brown colour

Limestone Decomposition
CaCO₃(s) 🔥 Heat → CaO(s) + CO₂(g)
Lead Nitrate Decomposition
2Pb(NO₃)₂(s) 🔥 Heat → 2PbO(s) + 4NO₂(g) + O₂(g)

Electrolytic Decomposition

When decomposition is carried out by passing electricity.

Water Electrolysis
2H₂O(l) ⚡ Electric current → 2H₂(g) + O₂(g)

Photolytic Decomposition

When decomposition is carried out in presence of sunlight.

Silver Chloride Decomposition
2AgCl(s) ☀️ Sunlight → 2Ag(s) + Cl₂(g)

Silver chloride turns grey on exposure to sunlight

Silver Bromide Decomposition
2AgBr(s) ☀️ Sunlight → 2Ag(s) + Br₂(g)

📸 Used in black and white photography

🔥 Endothermic Reactions

Decomposition reactions are mostly endothermic in nature. Energy in the form of heat, light or electricity is generally absorbed in these reactions.

Important Questions for Exams

1
What is a combination reaction? Give three examples with balanced chemical equations.
A combination reaction is one in which two or more reactants combine to form a single product.

Examples:
1. C(s) + O₂(g) → CO₂(g)
2. 2H₂(g) + O₂(g) → 2H₂O(l)
3. CaO(s) + H₂O(l) → Ca(OH)₂(aq)
2
Explain the three types of decomposition reactions with examples.
1. Thermal Decomposition: Decomposition by heating
Example: 2FeSO₄(s) → Fe₂O₃(s) + SO₂(g) + SO₃(g)

2. Electrolytic Decomposition: Decomposition by electricity
Example: 2H₂O(l) → 2H₂(g) + O₂(g)

3. Photolytic Decomposition: Decomposition by sunlight
Example: 2AgCl(s) → 2Ag(s) + Cl₂(g)
3
What are exothermic and endothermic reactions? Give examples.
Exothermic Reactions: Reactions that release heat energy
Example: CH₄(g) + 2O₂(g) → CO₂(g) + 2H₂O(g) + Heat

Endothermic Reactions: Reactions that absorb heat energy
Example: CaCO₃(s) + Heat → CaO(s) + CO₂(g)
4
Why is respiration considered an exothermic reaction?
Respiration is considered exothermic because it releases energy in the form of heat. During respiration, glucose reacts with oxygen to produce carbon dioxide, water, and energy:

C₆H₁₂O₆(aq) + 6O₂(g) → 6CO₂(g) + 6H₂O(l) + Energy

This energy is used by our body for various metabolic activities.
5
What happens when silver chloride is exposed to sunlight? Write the balanced equation.
When silver chloride is exposed to sunlight, it decomposes to form silver and chlorine gas. The silver formed appears grey in color.

2AgCl(s) + Sunlight → 2Ag(s) + Cl₂(g)

This reaction is used in black and white photography.
